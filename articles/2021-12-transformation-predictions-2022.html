Transformation Predictions for 2022

Tony Fergusson22 Dec 2021
ArticleUK Tech News

As 2021 draws to a close, we find ourselves at an inflection point for enterprise security and digital transformation. The pandemic accelerated many trends that were already underway — cloud adoption, remote work, zero trust architecture — but it also exposed deep vulnerabilities in organisations that had deferred security modernisation for too long.

Prediction 1: Zero Trust Moves from Concept to Mandate

Zero trust has been discussed as a security philosophy for over a decade. In 2022, I believe we will see it transition from aspiration to requirement. The Biden administration's May 2021 Executive Order on Improving the Nation's Cybersecurity set a clear mandate for US federal agencies, and European governments and enterprises have been paying close attention. The question is no longer whether to adopt zero trust, but how quickly.

Prediction 2: Ransomware Becomes an Existential Concern

The Colonial Pipeline attack, the Kaseya VSA breach, the JBS Foods ransomware incident — 2021 demonstrated that ransomware is no longer merely a financial nuisance. It is an existential threat to critical infrastructure, supply chains, and the organisations that depend on them. In 2022, boards will be forced to engage with ransomware risk at the same level they engage with financial and reputational risk. CISOs who can speak the language of business risk, not just technical vulnerability, will be in high demand.

Prediction 3: The Workforce Security Challenge Intensifies

Hybrid working is here to stay, and with it comes a permanent expansion of the enterprise attack surface. The days of securing a well-defined perimeter are behind us. In 2022, organisations will need to invest heavily in securing not just their corporate endpoints, but the home networks, personal devices, and third-party SaaS applications that their workforce uses daily. Identity will become the new perimeter.

Prediction 4: Supply Chain Security Rises to the Top of the Agenda

SolarWinds shocked the security community with the revelation that sophisticated attackers could use trusted software updates as a delivery mechanism for malware. In 2022, supply chain security will receive investment commensurate with its risk level. Third-party risk management — historically the poor cousin of enterprise security — will get the resources and attention it deserves.

2022 will not be an easy year for security practitioners. But the challenges ahead are navigable for organisations willing to be honest about their exposures and determined to close the gaps.
