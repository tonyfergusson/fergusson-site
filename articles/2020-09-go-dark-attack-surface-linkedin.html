Go Dark: Reduce Your Attack Surface

Tony Fergusson04 Sep 2020
ArticleLinkedIn

I started this mission back in 2015, and now five years into it I am more convinced than ever that it is worthwhile and will continue to protect the enterprise far into the future. The most important and fundamental principle of the mission is securing your enterprise by reducing the attack surface.

This one principle will dramatically reduce the risk of being a victim of the next ransomware attack, or some data exfiltration campaign.

Going Dark on the Internet

The idea of making the internet or corporate network dark has gained momentum since the introduction of Software Defined Networking (SDN). This has enabled a new concept called Software Defined Perimeter (SDP) — or what some are referring to as a Black Cloud.

The Darknet might sound like Batman's Batcave network, but in fact it can serve as a means of decoupling network access from private application access. Rather than exposing your entire network to the internet, you expose nothing. Applications are hidden behind an always-on, always-verified access layer.

Hackers cannot attack what they cannot see. This is not a theoretical concept — it's a proven architecture that I implemented at MAN Energy Solutions, shielding 16,000 employees and hundreds of ship control systems from the internet entirely.

The Zero Trust Approach

Zero Trust Network Access (ZTNA) operationalises the "go dark" philosophy. Rather than granting network access and hoping users and devices behave well, ZTNA grants application-level access on a per-session, per-user, per-device basis — after verifying identity, health, and context.

Lock down your network doors and windows with ZTNA. Go dark. Hackers cannot attack what they cannot see.
